VEERUPAKSHAPPA VEERSHETTY MAHAGAON

(1911-2004)

Veerupakshappa Veersheety was a freedom fighter for Hyderabad-Karnataka distinct and a noble social worker. He was born in a poor family in Mahagaon, Gulbarga in 1911. He was very young when he started to search job, he was hardly 12 years old and did not find any job . Due to which he started to sell sweets. Once when he was selling sweets, few Muslim boys tool his sweets did not give his money back instead they hurt him. This was the time when he realized that a lot of injustice is happening due to “Razakar movement”. Along with his friend Chandrashekar patil decided to fight for this injustice happening in the society. They both along with their other friends formed a "Youth Sung", for which his friend Chandrashekar Patil was made leader. Then all sung members took a blood oath to get a freedom against the Nizams in any situation. They started doing "Satya Garah", due to which few members of Youth Sung were put behind the bars, in which even Veerupakshappa was also jailed. He was in prison for 9 months. Later, on 17th September 1948 Nizam was overthrown by force (that was after the India's Independence, which is now celebrated as Hyderabad-Karnataka Liberation day). After this, he also fought for equal educational rights for women in his region. He has done a lot of scarifies and contribution in his region. At last, he took his last breathe on 30 July 2004.
